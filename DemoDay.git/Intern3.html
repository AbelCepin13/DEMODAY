The Los Angeles Times High School Insider Summer Internship Program

The Los Angeles Times High School Insider Summer Internship Program presents a remarkable opportunity for high school students passionate about journalism to immerse themselves in the dynamic world of media and storytelling. This intensive program equips students with hands-on experience across various facets of journalism, including reporting, writing, editing, and multimedia production. Under the guidance of seasoned professionals, interns gain a profound understanding of journalistic practices, ethics, and the significance of accurate, impactful reporting. Through mentorship, workshops, and collaborative newsroom experiences, participants develop essential skills that pave the way for future careers in journalism. The program encourages students to explore their interests, hone their storytelling techniques, and navigate the evolving landscape of digital media. By facilitating direct engagement with newsroom operations, the Los Angeles Times High School Insider Summer Internship Program nurtures the talents of aspiring journalists, fostering a new generation of voices that contribute to insightful and responsible storytelling. This program not only enriches students' knowledge but also promotes critical thinking, effective communication, and a broader understanding of current events. The experience empowers young minds to leverage their voices in the pursuit of truth and serves as a stepping stone toward potential careers in journalism and media-related fields.

Pasquines Writing Internship

The Pasquines Writing Internship offers an exceptional avenue for emerging writers to delve into the realm of political and social commentary. Catering to the Puerto Rican and Latinx communities, this internship provides interns with a platform to amplify underrepresented voices and address pressing issues. Through insightful articles, thoughtful analyses, and informed perspectives, interns contribute to Pasquines, an online publication that strives to inform, engage, and advocate. Interns engage in rigorous research, honing their ability to dissect complex subjects and convey them effectively to a diverse readership. The internship cultivates critical thinking skills, strengthens research capabilities, and fosters nuanced understanding of socio-political dynamics. Participants become part of a supportive community that values diverse viewpoints and encourages impactful dialogue. By participating in the Pasquines Writing Internship, young writers not only refine their writing skills but also learn how to use their words to shape conversations, challenge norms, and inspire change. This experience transcends traditional internships, offering a platform where passionate voices can resonate and contribute to meaningful societal discussions.

Building-U Writing Internship

The Building-U Writing Internship caters to individuals with an interest in architecture, design, and urban planning. This immersive experience allows interns to delve into creating content that explores the intersection of these fields with everyday life. Through compelling articles, interns communicate the significance of architecture and design in shaping societies, aesthetics, and functionality. Interns refine their writing skills while gaining a comprehensive understanding of architectural concepts and their real-world applications. The internship fosters an appreciation for the built environment's impact on communities, sustainability, and cultural identity. Participants develop the ability to bridge technical jargon with accessible language, making intricate ideas relatable to a broader audience. By participating in the Building-U Writing Internship, aspiring writers not only expand their portfolios but also contribute to fostering a deeper awareness of how architecture and design influence our surroundings. The program's focus on effective communication and insightful exploration ensures that interns emerge with a unique skill set that can transcend various industries.

Benjamin Lawless Internship at The Smithsonian Office of Internship Programs

The Benjamin Lawless Internship, hosted by The Smithsonian Office of Internship Programs, provides an exceptional opportunity for individuals passionate about cultural preservation, education, and outreach. Through diverse projects, participants engage with the inner workings of one of the world's most renowned cultural institutions. From assisting in research endeavors to contributing to event planning, interns play a pivotal role in supporting The Smithsonian's mission. Interns gain firsthand experience in the fields of history, art, science, and culture. They collaborate with experts, curators, and professionals across disciplines, broadening their understanding of the role museums play in education and public engagement. The internship facilitates exposure to the behind-the-scenes efforts that bring exhibits, programs, and resources to life. By participating in the Benjamin Lawless Internship, individuals not only contribute to The Smithsonian's impactful work but also cultivate skills that extend beyond the museum sphere. The experience offers a multifaceted perspective on cultural stewardship and prepares interns for careers in fields related to research, education, and public engagement.
