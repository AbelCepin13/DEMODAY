Brooklyn Museum | Teen Programs

The Brooklyn Museum provides a dynamic platform through its Teen Programs, offering an array of opportunities for young minds to engage with art and creativity. From workshops and exhibitions to special events, these programs are designed to nurture artistic skills, critical thinking, and self-expression among teenagers. Participants can explore various mediums, connect with like-minded peers, and gain insights into the art world. Through hands-on experiences, teens are encouraged to develop their own artistic voices, building a strong foundation for future endeavors in the arts.

Metropolitan Museum of Art High School Internships

The Metropolitan Museum of Art offers immersive High School Internships, granting students unique access to the museum's diverse departments. This program provides an exceptional chance to learn from professionals in fields like curation, education, conservation, and more. Through mentorship and engaging projects, students gain practical insights into museum operations and art history, enhancing their knowledge and skills in the process. This opportunity not only deepens their understanding of art but also prepares them for potential careers within cultural institutions.

Museum of Fine Arts, Boston | Teen Programs

The Museum of Fine Arts, Boston, hosts an array of Teen Programs designed to ignite creativity and foster connections among young individuals. Through workshops, art-making sessions, and interactions with artists, teenagers can explore their artistic passions and develop a lasting appreciation for art and culture. These programs provide a space for teens to engage in meaningful discussions, collaborate on projects, and gain exposure to diverse artistic perspectives. By actively participating in these programs, teenagers can discover their artistic potential and contribute to a vibrant creative community.

Art Institute of Chicago | Opportunities for Teens

The Art Institute of Chicago presents an array of Opportunities for Teens, aimed at nurturing artistic exploration and skill development. These opportunities encompass workshops, exhibitions, and mentorship programs that encourage teens to express themselves creatively and deepen their understanding of art. By participating, teenagers can connect with professional artists, engage with thought-provoking exhibitions, and refine their artistic techniques. These experiences empower young artists to build their portfolios, develop critical thinking skills, and cultivate a lifelong passion for the arts.

Getty Museum Student Gallery Guides

The Getty Museum's Student Gallery Guides program offers a unique educational experience for students to actively engage with art. This initiative allows participants to research and create guided tours for their peers, honing their public speaking, research, and interpretive skills. By delving into the stories behind artworks and sharing their insights, students not only deepen their understanding of art history but also enhance their ability to communicate complex ideas. This program fosters a strong connection between students and art, enabling them to appreciate and interpret artworks with newfound depth and perspective.

Dallas Museum of Art | Teen Programs

The Dallas Museum of Art provides an enriching array of Teen Programs designed to empower young individuals to explore, create, and connect through art. Workshops, discussions, and collaborative projects encourage creative expression, critical thinking, and dialogue among teens, fostering a sense of community within the art world. These programs offer a platform for teenagers to voice their perspectives and engage with artworks from diverse cultures and time periods. By participating, teens can develop their artistic skills, broaden their cultural awareness, and contribute meaningfully to the art community.
