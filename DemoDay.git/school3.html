Brooklyn Technical High School

Brooklyn Technical High School, often referred to as Brooklyn Tech, stands as a beacon of academic excellence in the heart of Brooklyn. Renowned nationally, it offers a specialized curriculum with a strong emphasis on science, technology, engineering, and mathematics (STEM) disciplines. With a diverse student body, rigorous coursework, and competitive admissions, Brooklyn Tech provides a dynamic learning environment for talented individuals to thrive. Graduates of Brooklyn Tech often excel in STEM-related fields, contributing to groundbreaking research and innovation.

Edward R. Murrow High School

Edward R. Murrow High School, situated in the diverse neighborhood of Midwood, Brooklyn, prioritizes academic achievement and holistic development. With specialized programs in arts, humanities, and media studies, the school empowers students to explore their passions while engaging in rigorous coursework. Murrow's commitment to fostering a supportive community encourages students to excel academically and creatively, preparing them for both college and career pathways.

Midwood High School

Midwood High School, located in the Flatbush neighborhood, is dedicated to academic excellence and Advanced Placement (AP) coursework. With a broad spectrum of programs, including medical science, humanities, and business, the school offers a comprehensive education tailored to diverse student interests. Midwood's commitment to intellectual growth, combined with a variety of extracurricular activities, fosters a well-rounded educational experience.

Leon M. Goldstein High School for the Sciences

Leon M. Goldstein High School for the Sciences, situated in the Sheepshead Bay neighborhood, specializes in science-related pathways. With programs focusing on medical science, research, and more, the school prepares students for future careers in STEM fields. Through rigorous coursework and research opportunities, Goldstein High School cultivates critical thinking and scientific inquiry.

Brooklyn Latin School

Brooklyn Latin School, located in the Bedford-Stuyvesant neighborhood, offers a classical liberal arts curriculum with a focus on language, literature, and history. Through a curriculum inspired by the classics, the school fosters an appreciation for the humanities and critical reading. With an emphasis on language proficiency and analytical thinking, Brooklyn Latin School equips students with skills applicable to a range of academic pursuits.

Millennium Brooklyn High School

Millennium Brooklyn High School, situated in the Prospect Heights neighborhood, stands at the intersection of education and social justice. Through project-based learning and community engagement, the school prepares students to be active citizens who address societal challenges. With a focus on collaboration, critical thinking, and global awareness, Millennium Brooklyn High School empowers students to create positive change in their communities.
